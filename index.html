Nikola Tesla

The man responible for designing the alternating-current (AC) electric system.

“If you want to find the secrets of the universe, think in terms of energy, frequency and vibration.”

  • 1856 - Born in Smiljan, Croatia.
  • 1862 - Tesla family moves to Gospić, because his father recieves appointment as pastor to a church.
  • 1863 - Tesla's older brother dies from a horse.
  • 1875 - Tesla enrolled at the Austrian Polytechnic School in Graz on a Military Border scholarship. Tesla lost his scholarship and took up gambling during his second year. Tesla stopped attending lectures and dropped out the following year.
  • 1882 - Tesla, obsessed with solving the riddle of A.C., suffers a mental breakdown and isn't expected to live. Tesla begins to recover and during a walk, the solution comes to tesla in a vision; he drew a diagram of the motor in the sand with a stick. He also went to go work for Edison.
  • 1885 - After Edison refused to pay, Tesla quit working for Edison, with the plan and intention of starting his own electrical company.
  • 1891 - Tesla patented the Tesla Coil; Tesla's alternating current powered the World's Columbian Exhibition.
  • 1901 - Tesla partners with J.P. Morgan, signs over 51 percent interest in his patents and inventions, including future ones, relating to both electric lighting and wireless telegraphy or telephony for a mere $150,000.00.
  • 1943 - Tesla dies at 86.
